A good trip plan is not created from one perfect prompt. It is created from context.

OurTrips now starts from the beginning of the trip, so you can collect that context before the itinerary is generated. The travel agent can work from the real material: what is already booked, what you care about, what your group needs, and what is still undecided.

Here is the workflow.

## 1. Start with the shape of the trip

Open OurTrips and start a new trip. The first questions are deliberately simple:

- Destination
- Dates
- Travelers
- Origin
- Budget
- Pace
- Must-do places
- Things already booked

This gives the agent the frame. A five-day trip with two people, one booked hotel, and a slow pace should become a different plan from an eight-day family route with three stays and a rental car.

## 2. Add the messy material

This is the part most itinerary tools skip.

Bring the information you already have. Paste the restaurant list. Add the hotel confirmation details. Include the ferry timing you found. Mention the friend recommendation that sounds promising but not essential. Add notes like "we need a rest after lunch" or "avoid changing hotels too often."

The goal is not to make the input beautiful. The goal is to make it complete enough that the plan respects reality.

Useful material includes:

- Flight and train times
- Hotel names, check-in notes, and booking status
- Restaurant links and reservation notes
- Ticket times and activity constraints
- Neighborhood preferences
- Mobility, energy, and pace notes
- Old plans, PDFs, and pasted reference material

## 3. Let OurTrips create the first draft

Once the trip has enough context, OurTrips creates a starter guide. The agent organizes the material into days, checks the route shape, adds practical tips, and keeps open decisions visible.

The first draft is not meant to be sacred. It is meant to be useful.

It gives you something to react to. You can see whether a day is too full, whether two places are awkwardly far apart, whether the hotel choice changes the rhythm of the trip, and whether the plan actually sounds like your group.

## 4. Refine the real itinerary

The trip chat is attached to the itinerary, so follow-up edits happen against the guide itself.

You can ask:

> Make Day 2 less packed and move one museum to Day 4.

Or:

> We booked Hotel des Arts. Update the stay and check whether nearby dinner choices still make sense.

Or:

> Add one casual lunch near the station before the train.

The important part is that the result is not buried in another chat answer. The guide updates.

## 5. Carry the trip with you

Before you leave, share the trip with co-travelers and save it offline. On the road, open the day you are in. The guide should answer the practical questions quickly: what is next, where is it, what is booked, where you sleep, and what still needs attention.

That is the advantage of collecting the messy material upfront. The final guide is not a generic itinerary. It is your trip, in a format designed to move with you.

## The connector is still there

If you already planned somewhere else, you can still connect an external agent through `https://ourtrips.to/mcp` and send the trip into OurTrips.

But for most people, the cleaner path is now simpler: start in OurTrips, add the pile, and let the guide take shape.
